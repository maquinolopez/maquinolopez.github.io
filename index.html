About Me

Originally from Oaxaca, Mexico, I am a statistician passionate about solving complex quantitative problems. My academic background spans mathematics and statistics, culminating in a PhD from Queen's University Belfast, where I developed Bayesian statistical methods for age-depth modeling of paleoclimate records, with a particular focus on radiocarbon and lead-210 dating.

My research centers on improving uncertainty quantification in paleoenvironmental reconstructions and chronological alignment. I have held research positions at the University of Cambridge, Queen’s University Belfast, and Maynooth University, and I am currently an Associate Researcher at CIMAT in Guanajuato, Mexico.

I actively contribute to the development of open-source statistical tools and collaborate across disciplines—bridging environmental science, Bayesian modeling, and computational statistics.
